A Commitment

Inspired by a post by David Drake, I've decided to follow in his footsteps, but in my own way.

The prevailing thought is that a lunch break might be best served as a period of time to rest your brain from your work. Eat your lunch, maybe chat with a friend, but otherwise just rewind and enjoy your food. There's some wisdom out there that your overall productivity is higher by separating yourself from your work for a time.

So, I'm going to stop eating lunch at my desk. But instead of emptying my mind, I want to stay productive in a different way, so I'm committing myself to reading technical books.

Here's the schedule, and hopefully I'll find time to blog about each one. I'll start off January with some well-regarded general knowledge Javascript books, and end the month with some Javascript books with a more functional slant as I move into fully functional February.

January

  • Maintainable JavaScript
  • Effective JavaScript
  • JavaScript Allonge
  • Functional JavaScript: Introducing Functional Programming with Underscore.js

February

  • Programming Clojure
  • Clojure Programming
  • The Joy of Clojure

I'm excited.
